Trading Standards issue fresh warning over doorstep traders targeting Worcestershire residents

Officers say complaints about rogue builders, roofers and landscapers have risen sharply this year, with cold-call scams accounting for the majority of reported cases across the district.
Consumer By Daniel Marsh, Consumer Affairs 4 min read

Worcestershire Trading Standards has issued a fresh warning about doorstep traders following a sharp rise in complaints from residents across the county, with Bromsgrove district recording some of the highest numbers of reported incidents.

Officers say the majority of complaints involve traders who cold-call at residential properties offering building, roofing or garden work, often claiming to have spotted a problem while working nearby.

In many cases, residents who engage with these traders are pressured into making immediate cash payments before any work begins. In the most serious cases, money is taken and the trader does not return, leaving homeowners out of pocket and with work either unstarted or incomplete.

"If someone arrives at your door uninvited offering to carry out work on your home, the safest answer is always no."

Worcestershire Trading Standards

Trading Standards is advising residents never to agree to work from an uninvited trader on the doorstep, to always obtain written quotes from at least three contractors before committing, and to verify a trader's credentials independently before handing over any money.

Officers also recommend asking traders whether they have a VIA number and verifying it at viacheck.co.uk, which independently audits tradespeople's insurance, accreditations and business details.

Residents who believe they have been targeted by a rogue trader are asked to report the matter to Trading Standards via the Citizens Advice consumer helpline, and to contact West Mercia Police if money has been taken.
