Alexandra Hospital A&E wait times among worst in Midlands, figures show

New NHS data reveals average waits of over four hours at Redditch, with Bromsgrove residents bearing the brunt as the nearest major emergency department.
Health By Tom Hollis 4 min read

The Alexandra Hospital in Redditch recorded average accident and emergency waiting times of four hours and twenty-two minutes in the most recent monthly figures, placing it in the bottom fifth of trusts nationally.

The data, published by NHS England, covers the period from October to December and shows that only 61 per cent of patients at the Alexandra were seen within the four-hour target, against a national standard of 95 per cent.

The figures are of particular concern to Bromsgrove residents, for whom the Alexandra is the nearest major emergency department following the downgrading of services at Worcestershire Royal Hospital.

"Four hours is not acceptable. People are making decisions about whether to attend at all, and that is dangerous."

Local GP, Bromsgrove

A Bromsgrove GP who asked not to be named said the waiting times were leading some patients to delay seeking emergency care, which posed a risk to outcomes in time-sensitive conditions such as stroke and cardiac events.

Worcestershire Acute Hospitals NHS Trust, which runs the Alexandra, said demand had increased significantly over the winter period and acknowledged that performance had fallen short of the required standard.

The trust said it was working with Integrated Care Board partners to increase the capacity of urgent treatment centres and reduce avoidable attendances at the emergency department.
