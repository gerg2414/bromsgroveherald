Crime

Police appeal after catalytic converter thefts triple across the district

West Mercia Police have issued advice to motorists amid a spike in overnight thefts from hybrid vehicles, with Bromsgrove among the worst affected areas in the county.
Crime By Staff Reporter 4 min read
Hybrid vehicles are disproportionately targeted due to the higher precious metal content of their catalytic converters.

West Mercia Police have recorded a threefold increase in catalytic converter thefts across Bromsgrove district in the first quarter of this year, and are urging owners of hybrid vehicles in particular to take precautionary measures.

Officers say the thefts typically occur between midnight and 4am, with perpetrators using battery-powered cutting equipment to remove the converters in under three minutes. In several cases, residents living adjacent to the vehicles have been unaware until the morning.

Hybrid vehicles are disproportionately targeted because their catalytic converters contain higher concentrations of platinum, palladium and rhodium than those fitted to conventional petrol or diesel engines.

"These thefts can happen in under two minutes. In many cases residents have slept through it entirely."

West Mercia Police, Bromsgrove District

Police are advising owners to park in well-lit areas or locked garages where possible, to consider fitting a catalytic converter protection plate, and to mark their converter with a forensic marking kit.

A number of local car parks have installed additional CCTV following liaison with the police, and officers have increased overnight patrols in residential areas identified as hotspots.

Anyone with information about catalytic converter thefts in the district is asked to contact West Mercia Police on 101 or report anonymously through Crimestoppers.
